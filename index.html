The Quick Brown Fox and the Art of Reading

Reading is something most people take for granted. The brain decodes symbols on a page and transforms them into meaning — almost instantly, and without effort. But for millions of people, that process works differently. Words shift. Letters blur. The page becomes noise.

Dyslexia affects roughly one in five people. It has nothing to do with intelligence. Many people with dyslexia are highly creative, strategic thinkers — they simply process written language through a different path. The right support, tools, and understanding can make all the difference.

This simulator cannot fully replicate what it's like to have dyslexia. But spending a few minutes here might help you understand why the right font, spacing, and design choices matter more than most people realize.
